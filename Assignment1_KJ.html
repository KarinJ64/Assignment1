Biography

Education

I have a master of science in Electrical Engineering from KTH with a specialization in radio and telecoms. Note that this was before mobile telephony and cellular networks were widely spread, so our focus was mainly on fixed telephony and fixed radio.

Personal life

I am married with and have two children. My daughter, aged 30, is a linguist, having studied computer linguistics and nordic languages (Danish, Icelandic) at Uppsala University. My son, aged 28, has a masters degree in information technology from Uppsala University, and is currently trying to get employeed, which seems as far more of a challenge today than it was when I finished my education in 1987.

Work experience

I have worked in several companies with very different tasks. My career started in 1987 with research around signal processing and transform methods for the Swedish military.

In 1991 I joined Ericsson, to which I have come back several times, as it is one of the bigger employers in Stockholm, especially if you have a background in telecoms. My first role was to travel to Ericsson's customers across the world, teaching them about mobile telephony in general, and Ericsson's systems in particular. GSM was introduced in Sweden in 1991 and it was a very interesting time in the early 90's with the introduction of mobile telephony on a broader scale across the globe. I traveled to Australia, US, Singapore and all across Europe with my slide deck (very heavy) and course books (and no laptop or mobile phone).

I then moved on to more sales related tasks such as building business case models for simulation of revenues and costs related to introduction of 3G, all in order to prepare Ericsson's customers when building their analyses on what to pay for the 3G licenses, primarily in Europe.

Then the dotcom bubble burst and also Ericsson had issues, but before that I had, luckily, moved to a new position, working with an operator and their 3G introduction in Sweden. This was the first time I came in contact with proucurement, which since then has become by main work area.

After a few tough years in the telecom industry during the early 2000, I rejoined Ericsson in 2007, focusing only on procurement, or sourcing as we call it in Ericsson. I first worked five years with IT sourcing, where my main task was to re-negotiate the outsourcing agreement of IT infrastructure (laptops, servers, storage) for Ericsson's 100 000 employees. Thereafter I joined the Hardware Sourcing unit where I am currently employeed, where we source all components needed to manufacture Ericsson's products (primarily radio and baseband units). My current category is PCBs (printed circuit boards), where all our suppliers are based either in China or in Taiwan. Naturally this means a lot of international contacts, even though the level of travel has decreased significantly after Covid-19 and the economic downturn.
